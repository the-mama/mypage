# Data Engineering Interview Preparation Guide

## What It Is

A structured approach to preparing for data engineering interviews, covering system design, SQL, and behavioral questions.

## Why It's Useful

Most interview prep is generic. This is specific to data engineering—the questions you'll actually face.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. System Design (30-40% of interview)

**What they ask:**
- "Design a data pipeline for X"
- "How would you handle 1 billion events per day?"
- "Design a real-time analytics system"

**What to prepare:**
- Trade-offs (speed vs. accuracy, cost vs. complexity)
- Common tools (Spark, Kafka, Snowflake, dbt)
- Scalability considerations
- Data quality concerns

**Practice:**
- Draw architecture diagrams
- Discuss trade-offs out loud
- Practice explaining your choices to non-technical people

### 2. SQL & Transformations (30-40%)

**What they ask:**
- "Write a query to find X"
- "Optimize this query"
- "Transform this data shape"

**What to prepare:**
- Window functions
- CTEs and recursive queries
- Performance optimization
- Normalization and denormalization

**Practice:**
- LeetCode SQL problems (medium difficulty)
- Write queries without looking them up first
- Explain your approach before coding

### 3. Behavioral & Experience (20-30%)

**What they ask:**
- "Tell me about your biggest project"
- "How did you handle a production issue?"
- "Describe a time you failed"

**What to prepare:**
- STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Quantify impact (reduced latency from 5s to 200ms)
- Show growth and learning
- Be honest about challenges

## Interview Week Timeline

**2 weeks before:**
- Review system design fundamentals
- Practice 10-15 SQL problems
- Prepare 3-4 strong project examples

**1 week before:**
- Mock interview with a friend
- Review common data engineering patterns
- Get good sleep (seriously)

**Day of:**
- Eat breakfast
- Arrive 10 minutes early
- Breathe deeply
- Remember: they want you to succeed

## Red Flags vs. Green Flags

**You know you're ready when:**
- You can design a system without freezing
- You can explain SQL queries in plain English
- You have stories that show growth

**You need more prep if:**
- You panic at unfamiliar tools
- You can't explain your own projects
- You've only memorized solutions

## Final Advice

Interviews are a two-way street. You're evaluating them too.

Ask about:
- Data volume and complexity
- Team structure and growth
- Technical challenges they're facing
- How they handle on-call and incident response
