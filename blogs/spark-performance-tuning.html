Spark Job Performance Tuning

Transforming 4-hour jobs into 12 minutes — practical tuning strategies that work

April 2024 • Spark, Databricks, Performance Optimization

The Cost of Slow Spark Jobs

A data team ran a daily aggregation job that took 4 hours. Every delay in the morning meant dashboards weren't ready for stakeholders. The job consumed 8 r5.4xlarge nodes on Databricks—costing $500/day or $150k/year. After tuning, it ran in 12 minutes on 2 nodes. Cost: $12/day. Savings: $140k/year.

Performance tuning isn't a nice-to-have. It's a business decision.

Diagnosing the Problem: Spark UI

Never tune blindly. Always start with the Spark UI.

  • Jobs Tab: Which stages are slow? (Look for skewed stage duration)
  • Stages Tab: Task-level metrics. Is it I/O, CPU, or memory?
  • Executors Tab: Are executors memory-pressured? Spilling to disk?
  • SQL Tab: How many records per stage? Unexpected data movement?

Common Issues & Fixes

1. Skewed Partitions (The Most Common Problem)

One partition has 10B rows, others have 100M. That partition becomes the bottleneck.

Diagnosis: In Spark UI, Stages tab shows uneven task duration.

Fix: Repartition aggressively

df.repartition(200, "customer_id")\
  .groupBy("customer_id", "date")\
  .agg(sum("amount"))

Then, salt the join key to further distribute hot keys: 

val salted = df.withColumn("salt", (rand() * 10).cast("int"))
val joined = salted.join(
  other_df.withColumn("salt", explode(array((0 to 9).map(lit(_)): _*))),
  Seq("key", "salt"), "left"
).drop("salt")

2. Unnecessary Shuffles

Every groupBy, join, or window causes a shuffle. Multiple shuffles = multiple network transfers.

Fix: Combine operations to reduce shuffles

-- BAD: 2 shuffles
df.groupBy("id").agg(sum("sales"))
  .join(df.groupBy("id").agg(count("*")), "id")

-- GOOD: 1 shuffle
df.groupBy("id").agg(
  sum("sales").as("total_sales"),
  count("*").as("count")
)

3. Broadcast Join Missing

Joining a 100MB table with a 50GB table triggers a shuffle. If the 100MB table fits in memory, broadcast it instead. 

from pyspark.sql.functions import broadcast

small_df = spark.read.table("dim_product")  # 100MB
large_df = spark.read.table("fact_sales")    # 50GB

joined = large_df.join(broadcast(small_df), "product_id")

4. Too Many Small Files (Listing Bottleneck)

10M small Parquet files take minutes just to list. Querying takes hours.

Fix: Coalesce output

df.coalesce(100).write.mode("overwrite").parquet("s3://my-bucket/data")

5. String Operations (Expensive in Spark)

split("col", ","), regex, UDFs on strings are slow. 

-- SLOW:
SELECT split(data, ",")[0] as first_value

-- FASTER:
SELECT substring_index(data, ",", 1) as first_value

Real-World Tuning Example

A transaction processing job transformed 2B daily records. Original runtime: 4.5 hours.

  • Problem 1: Missing broadcast on 200MB dimension table → added broadcast join (30 min saved)
  • Problem 2: Double-grouped aggregation → combined into single groupBy (1 hr 15 min saved)
  • Problem 3: Skewed customer IDs (0.5% of IDs = 30% of rows) → salted key before join (45 min saved)
  • Problem 4: 50M tiny Parquet files → coalesced to 500 files (15 min saved)

Result: 4.5 hours → 12 minutes.

Databricks-Specific Tuning

  • Delta Cache: Enable SSD caching for hot tables. 10x I/O speedup.
  • Adaptive Query Execution: spark.sql.adaptive.enabled true. Auto-tunes partitions at runtime.
  • Liquid Clustering: Better than partitioning for skewed data. (Databricks Delta 12.0+)

Monitoring & Alerting

After tuning, don't let regressions creep in. 

-- Log job metrics
SELECT
  job_name,
  run_date,
  runtime_minutes,
  data_processed_gb,
  cost_per_gb
FROM job_metrics
ORDER BY run_date DESC

Key Takeaways

  • Always start with Spark UI. Guessing is expensive
  • Skew is the #1 performance killer. Repartition and salt aggressively
  • Combine operations to reduce shuffles
  • Broadcast small tables. It's free speed
  • Monitor after tuning. Regression detection is critical

Slow Spark jobs are a symptom of not looking at the data. Understand your data distribution, and the tuning becomes obvious.
