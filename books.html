Personal Growth

Books I Read

Transformative reads that shaped my thinking about life, spirituality, and data engineering

Autobiography of a Yogi

By Paramahansa Yogananda

A spiritual masterpiece that reveals the life of Sri Ramakrishna and his disciples in India. This book opened my eyes to meditation, the power of the mind, and the possibility of transcendence beyond material pursuits.

Key Takeaway: Discipline of mind through meditation is the foundation for all achievement. Success without inner peace is hollow.

Death: An Inside Story

By Sadhguru

Sadhguru explores the philosophy of death, not as an ending, but as a doorway to understanding life itself. This book reframed my perspective on mortality and what truly matters in our limited time.

Key Takeaway: Understanding death teaches you how to live. Most people live in illusion; awareness of mortality breaks that illusion.

The Mahabharata

By Vyasa (Condensed Version)

An ancient epic that weaves philosophy, ethics, and human drama into one narrative. The Bhagavad Gita within it—where Krishna teaches Arjuna about duty and detachment—became a personal compass.

Key Takeaway: Do your duty without attachment to the results. This principle applies to work, relationships, and personal growth.

Autobiography of a Himalayan Master

By Swami Rama

The life journey of Swami Rama—a meditation master who lived in caves, mastered the body, and brought Eastern wisdom to the West. His story proves that transformation is possible through dedicated practice and surrender.

Key Takeaway: The mind can be trained like any skill. Meditation is not mystical—it's practical technology for inner evolution.

Why I Read These

As a data engineer, I live in a world of systems, optimization, and logic. But these books reminded me that the external world is just a projection of the internal state. A disciplined mind is the greatest asset—more valuable than any skill or technology.

I read one book every month, focusing on philosophy, spirituality, and personal transformation. These choices shaped how I approach challenges, manage stress, and define success.
