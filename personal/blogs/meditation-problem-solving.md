# Meditation Changed How I Solve Problems

## The Setting

I started meditating because I was stressed. 45 minutes every morning, sitting, breathing, doing nothing. My colleagues thought I was wasting time.

Then something shifted.

## The Challenge

I used to approach problems like a firefighter—react fast, solve immediately, move on. But some problems aren't fires. Some are slow-burning issues that need patience and perspective.

A production database was degrading slowly. Everyone wanted a quick fix. I wanted to understand why.

## The Insight

Meditation taught me something counterintuitive: the best insights come when you stop trying.

I'd sit with the problem, not frantically coding. Not googling solutions. Just thinking. And somehow, clarity would emerge.

The root cause was always obvious in hindsight. But I'd never see it if my mind was racing.

### What Changed

- **Before meditation:** React → Fix → Next problem
- **After meditation:** Pause → Observe → Understand → Solve

Before, I'd solve the symptom. Now I solve the cause.

## How It Shapes Me Today

I meditate because it makes me a better engineer. Clear mind = clear code.

When I'm stuck on a hard problem:
- I don't panic
- I don't code-thrash
- I sit, breathe, and let my mind work in the background
- Answers come

It's not mystical. It's just how attention works.
