# Why I Cold Shower Every Morning

## The Setting

It started as a challenge. 30 days of cold showers to build mental toughness. 

5+ years later, I haven't missed a morning.

## The Challenge

Day 1: Miserable. My body screamed. I jumped in, gasped, jumped out.

Day 3: Still miserable. My mind was racing: "Do I really need this?"

Day 10: Something shifted. It wasn't comfortable, but I stopped fighting it.

Day 30: I realized I wasn't doing it for the shock anymore. I was doing it for what it taught me.

## The Insight

**Cold showers aren't about the cold. They're about training your mind to do hard things before breakfast.**

Here's what happens:
- Your amygdala fires up (fight/flight)
- Your rational mind says "you don't need this"
- You take a breath and do it anyway
- You feel alive and unstoppable for the rest of the day

You've told your nervous system: "We can handle discomfort."

## The Transfer

This transfers everywhere:
- Difficult conversation with a stakeholder? You've done worse (cold shower).
- Shipping code that might break? You've already faced discomfort today.
- Admitting you don't know something? Small price after ice water.

It's not about the cold. It's about proving to yourself that you can do things that are hard.

## How It Shapes Me Today

Every morning, I get in the shower, my body tenses up, and I smile.

Because I know that:
- I won't be controlled by comfort
- I can face discomfort and move through it
- I start the day as a person who does hard things

The cold doesn't bother me anymore. That's not the point. The point is the discipline.
