# The Pragmatic Programmer

**By David Thomas & Andrew Hunt**

## What It's About

A collection of practical advice for software developers. From debugging to refactoring to career growth. Timeless and applicable to any technology.

## Why I Read It

I was writing code. I wasn't thinking about being a programmer. This book bridged that gap.

## Key Concepts

### Don't Repeat Yourself (DRY)
The principle is simple. The practice is hard. But once you internalize it, you can't unsee duplication.

### Fix Broken Windows
A small mess leads to a bigger mess. Clean code culture starts with one person saying "no, we do better."

### Your Knowledge is Your Capital
Stay curious. Learn deeply. A programmer who only knows one technology is fragile.

## The Best Part

The section on debugging. They explain it's not about tools. It's about thinking systematically. Find your assumptions, test them, iterate.

Changed how I approach every bug.

## Who Should Read This

- Junior developers (immediately)
- Senior developers (to remember why you care)
- Team leads (to build culture)
- Anyone who wants to be taken seriously as an engineer

## Why It Matters

Most books become outdated. This one hasn't. The principles are language-agnostic, platform-agnostic, decade-agnostic.
