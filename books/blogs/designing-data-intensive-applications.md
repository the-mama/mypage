# Designing Data-Intensive Applications

**By Martin Kleppmann**

## What It's About

A deep dive into the theory and practice of distributed systems. Covers databases, message queues, consistency models, and scalability patterns.

## Why I Read It

I was building data pipelines blindly—optimizing things without understanding the trade-offs. This book changed that.

## Key Concepts

### Consistency vs. Availability
The CAP theorem isn't just theory. It explains why your database behaves the way it does, and why you can't have everything.

### Replication Strategies
Master-slave? Multi-leader? Leaderless? Each has trade-offs. Understanding them prevents you from building fragile systems.

### Batch vs. Streaming
Not about technology. About how to think about data differently. Batch is simpler. Streaming is powerful. Pick wisely.

## The Best Part

Chapter 11 on stream processing. Suddenly, real-time data pipelines made sense. Not the code—the principles.

## Who Should Read This

- Anyone building data systems (mandatory)
- Backend engineers who want to understand databases
- Architects choosing between technologies
- Team leads explaining why "it's complicated"

## Impact on My Work

Before: "I'll use this database because it's popular"  
After: "I'll use this database because it matches our consistency requirements"

Same person. Different thinking.
