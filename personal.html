My Daily Routine

Success is built on consistency. Here's a typical day in my life—how I approach work, health, and growth.

Morning Routine (5:00 AM – 8:00 AM)

5:00 – 5:15 AM
Wake up, cold shower
Activates the nervous system. No phone for the first 30 minutes.
5:15 – 6:00 AM
Meditation (45 minutes)
Vipassana or Pranayama. This sets the tone for the entire day.
6:00 – 6:30 AM
Exercise or yoga
30 min strength training or 30 min vinyasa flow. Alternating.
6:30 – 7:00 AM
Breakfast
High protein, healthy fats. No sugar. Usually eggs, avocado, whole grain toast.
7:00 – 8:00 AM
Learning (30 min) + Planning (30 min)
Read research papers, blogs, or industry news. Then plan the day's priorities.

Work Hours (8:00 AM – 6:00 PM)

8:00 – 12:00 PM
Deep work (no meetings)
Core engineering work. Code reviews, architecture, writing, problem-solving. No Slack or email interruptions.
12:00 – 1:00 PM
Lunch break
Step away from the desk. Walk, eat mindfully, no screens.
1:00 – 3:00 PM
Meetings + collaboration
Design reviews, mentoring, pair programming, or team sync.
3:00 – 5:00 PM
Deep work or admin
Writing documentation, code optimization, or handling operational tasks.
5:00 – 6:00 PM
Review & plan tomorrow
Check progress against daily goals. Plan next day. Close laptop.

Evening Routine (6:00 PM – 10:00 PM)

6:00 – 7:00 PM
Transition time
Walk, podcasts, or quiet time. Let go of work stress.
7:00 – 8:00 PM
Dinner
Cook or prepare meals for the week. Eat slowly, no screens.
8:00 – 9:30 PM
Reading or creative work
Philosophy books, personal projects, writing, or journaling.
9:30 – 10:00 PM
Sleep prep
No screens. Dim lights. Reflective breathing. Bed by 10:00 PM.

Why This Routine Works

  • Meditation first: A calm mind makes better decisions. Engineering is problem-solving. Clarity matters more than speed.
  • Deep work blocks: Interruptions kill productivity. Two 4-hour blocks beat eight 1-hour blocks.
  • No screens before bed: Blue light disrupts melatonin. Bad sleep cascades into bad decisions the next day.
  • Consistency: The routine is rigid, but that's the point. Discipline builds freedom.
  • Balance: 50% engineering, 30% growth, 20% admin. Not perfect, but intentional.

Principles I Live By

Discipline is freedom. A structured day paradoxically gives you more freedom—no decision fatigue, more energy for what matters.

Small compounding wins. 1% better every day = 37x better in a year. I track it: lines of code, books read, meditations completed.

Health is the foundation. Without sleep, exercise, and nutrition, no amount of intelligence helps. I never skip the fundamentals.

Boredom is a feature. Most people run from boredom. I embrace it. Meditation, journaling, long walks—these are where insights emerge.

Growth Areas

I'm not perfect. I still struggle with:

  • Saying no to meetings (deep work gets sacrificed)
  • Perfectionism (good-enough code shipped beats perfect code never shipped)
  • Social media (occasional dopamine hits distract from long-term goals)

But I track these failures, learn, and adjust. That's the game.
